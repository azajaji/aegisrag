The university VPN allows authorized users to access internal resources from off-campus networks. All faculty, staff, and currently enrolled students are eligible for VPN access.

To request a VPN account, log in to the IT self-service portal and submit a 'VPN Access' ticket. Account provisioning typically takes one business day.

On Windows, install the GlobalProtect client from the IT downloads page and use 'vpn.university.edu' as the portal address. Authentication uses your standard university single sign-on credentials.

On macOS, install the GlobalProtect package and grant the system extension permission in System Settings under Privacy and Security; failure to grant this permission prevents the tunnel from establishing.

Multi-factor authentication is required for all VPN connections. The default second factor is a push notification through the university Authenticator app; SMS fallback is available but discouraged.

VPN sessions automatically time out after eight hours of activity or two hours of inactivity. Users should disconnect manually when leaving sensitive sessions.

Split tunneling is enabled by default, meaning only traffic destined for university subnets is routed through the VPN. Full-tunnel mode can be activated by selecting 'Force All Traffic' in the client settings.
