All data managed by the university is classified into four tiers: Public, Internal, Confidential, and Restricted, with handling requirements escalating at each tier.

Public data may be shared without restriction. Examples include published research, course catalogs, and press releases.

Internal data is intended for university members and is not for public release; examples include policies, internal directories, and meeting minutes.

Confidential data requires encryption at rest and in transit and is restricted to authorized users. Examples include student records, salary information, and unpublished research.

Restricted data is the most sensitive class and includes regulated personal data, medical records, and certain classified research projects; storage outside university-managed systems is prohibited.
