Undergraduate degrees require completion of at least 120 credit hours including all major requirements, a minimum of 30 credit hours in residence, and a cumulative GPA of at least 2.0.

Master's programs require a minimum of 30 credit hours of approved coursework and either a thesis or a comprehensive examination; the choice is program-specific.

Doctoral programs require completion of all coursework, a qualifying examination, a dissertation proposal defense, and a final dissertation defense.

Application for graduation must be filed at least one semester before the expected graduation date. Late applications incur a 250 SAR processing fee.

Honors designations (cum laude, magna cum laude, summa cum laude) are awarded based on the final cumulative GPA at thresholds of 3.50, 3.70, and 3.90 respectively.
