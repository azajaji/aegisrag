All research involving human participants must receive approval from the Institutional Review Board before any data collection begins. Retroactive approval is not permitted.

IRB applications must be submitted at least six weeks before the planned start date of data collection. Expedited review is available for minimal-risk studies and typically takes two weeks.

Informed consent forms must include the purpose of the research, voluntary nature of participation, expected duration, potential risks, expected benefits, and contact information of the principal investigator.

Research data collected from human participants must be stored on encrypted university servers for a minimum of five years after publication. Cloud storage outside the kingdom requires explicit IRB authorization.

Conflicts of interest exceeding 10,000 SAR in annual value must be disclosed in the Annual Conflict of Interest report submitted by January 31 each year.

Authorship on research publications requires substantial contribution to design, execution, or interpretation; mere provision of funding or facilities does not qualify for authorship.
