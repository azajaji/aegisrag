Academic integrity is foundational to the university's educational mission. All students must uphold honesty in coursework, examinations, and research activities.

Plagiarism is defined as the presentation of another person's words, ideas, or data as one's own without proper attribution. A first plagiarism offence results in a written warning and mandatory completion of the Academic Integrity Workshop within 30 days.

A second plagiarism offence within the same academic year results in failure of the course and a notation on the student's academic record that remains visible for two years.

Examination cheating, including unauthorized use of electronic devices, communication with other students, or possession of unauthorized materials, carries an automatic course failure and academic probation for one semester.

Collaboration on individual assignments without explicit instructor permission is treated as academic dishonesty. Students are encouraged to consult the instructor when uncertain about permitted collaboration.

Falsification of research data, including fabrication of experimental results or selective reporting of outcomes, is grounds for expulsion regardless of prior offences.

Appeals against academic integrity sanctions must be submitted in writing to the Office of the Registrar within 14 calendar days of the decision being communicated.

Faculty members are required to report suspected violations of academic integrity within five business days of discovery using the official incident form available on the registrar portal.
