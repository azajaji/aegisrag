Master's thesis defenses must be scheduled at least three weeks in advance and require a committee of three faculty members including the supervisor and one external examiner.

Doctoral dissertation defenses must be scheduled at least six weeks in advance and require a committee of five faculty members, two of whom must be external to the university.

The defense begins with a public presentation of approximately 30 minutes for master's students and 45 minutes for doctoral candidates, followed by closed-door questioning by the committee.

Possible defense outcomes are: pass without revision, pass with minor revisions to be completed within 30 days, pass with major revisions requiring resubmission within 90 days, or fail.

The defense outcome is determined by majority vote of the committee. In case of a tie the external examiner's vote is decisive.
