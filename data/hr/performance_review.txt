All employees undergo a formal performance review once per fiscal year, typically between March and May. The review covers the preceding twelve months of work.

The review uses a five-point scale: 1 (unsatisfactory), 2 (needs improvement), 3 (meets expectations), 4 (exceeds expectations), and 5 (outstanding).

Employees rated 1 or 2 are placed on a Performance Improvement Plan with measurable goals and a review date 90 days later. Failure to meet the plan may result in termination.

A rating of 4 or 5 makes the employee eligible for the annual merit increase, which is calibrated by the compensation committee in June and applied to July paychecks.

Employees may submit a written response to their review within ten business days. Disagreements are escalated to the second-level manager and HR.
